Day 1

The Indian Dance forms have their own language of expression. These dance forms have been used to tell stories, convey emotions and spread messages for a long time. Whether it is a classical dance or a folk dance, each performance tells a story. It came alive centuries ago and has been progressing ever since. It transcends every boundary of language. Many students have been practising such dance forms for years and should get a chance to express themselves. We, at The Heritage School, call upon such talented dancers to come forward and tell their stories.

THEME

Fusion of any Indian dance forms

RULES

1. Number of participants: 2+6 (Class 10-12)
2. Duration: 3+1 mins
3. Every team should include a short introduction of their performance in the video, stating the inspiration of the fusion, from where they got the idea behind their performance.
4. Points shall be deducted for exceeding the time limit.
5. The originality of the performance should remain intact.
6. Songs containing offensive language should not be used.
7. Costumes should be appropriate for a school environment.
8. Social distancing norms must be followed.

JUDGING CRITERIA

Expression
Choreography
Coordination
Presentation
